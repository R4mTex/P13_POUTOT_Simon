Legal notice for the web platform

This legal notice template groups together the information that a web platform must make available to its users.

What are the legal notices on a web platform?
This information is required by law for all web platforms. It must be accessible on a dedicated page and mainly includes information and contact details for the publisher and the host of the web platform.
Good to know: for non-professional web platforms, only the information is mandatory.

Publisher information
The publisher is the owner of the web platform. It is the one who operates and controls the content of the web platform and assumes responsibility for it. The legal notice must indicate:
For an individual (sole proprietorship, self-employed):

  • Name, first name, address, phone number, and email;
  • In case of registration with the Trade and Companies Register (RCS) or the Directory of Trades (RM), the registration number.
For a legal entity (company or association):
  • The official name, registered office address, phone number, and email;
  • For companies registered with the RCS or RM, the registration number and share capital;
  • The name of the publication director, i.e., the person who assumes criminal responsibility for everything published on the web platform. In principle, this is the legal representative (director) of the company or association.
Special cases:
  • For VAT-registered activities: the intra-community VAT number;
  • For activities subject to administrative authorization: the name and address of the authority that issued the authorization;
  • For regulated professions: reference to applicable professional rules, professional title, the country in which it was issued, and the name of the professional order or organization.

Host Information
The host is the service provider responsible for hosting the web platform and making it accessible online. The legal notice must indicate:

  • Its name or corporate name;
  • Its address and phone number.

Other Information
The legal notice serves to remind users that the web platform and its content are protected by intellectual property law, primarily copyright law.
The legal notice page may also contain information on the collection of personal data of users, as required by the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). However, it is generally recommended to consolidate this information on a dedicated page: we offer a Privacy Policy template for this purpose.
Good to know: in addition to the legal notice, e-commerce web platforms must publish general terms and conditions of sale (GTC). We offer a web platform General Terms and Conditions (TOS/GTC) template for this purpose.

How to use this document?
Once completed, the legal notices must be published and accessible on a dedicated page (usually through a link at the top or bottom of the homepage).

Applicable Law
Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR)
Law No. 2004-575 of June 21, 2004, for confidence in the digital economy
Law No. 78-17 of January 6, 1978, relating to data processing, files, and freedoms

Legal Assistance
You may choose to consult with a lawyer if you need assistance.
The lawyer can answer your questions or assist you in your procedures. This option will be offered to you at the end of the document.

How to Modify the Template?
